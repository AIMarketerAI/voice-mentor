# Legacy Blueprint — Interview for AI Mark's Knowledge Base

Answer in your own voice, as if talking to a friend — the raw material *is* the
product. Skip anything, answer in any order, add anything unasked. Write answers
directly under each question and we'll wire them into AI Mark's system prompt.

## A. Origin story
1. Who are you, in the way you'd introduce yourself to someone you like?
2. What was the lowest professional moment that shaped how you mentor?
3. What was the turning point — the decision or realisation that changed things?
4. What do you do now, and who do you do it for?
5. What would surprise people about your journey?

## B. Philosophy & method
6. What's the core belief behind how you help people think clearly?
7. When someone's stuck on an idea, what's the FIRST question you always ask?
8. What's your framework (formal or instinctive) for turning ideas into action?
9. What common advice do you think is wrong, and what do you say instead?
10. Describe a real client transformation you're proud of (anonymised is fine).

## C. Voice & tone
11. Three adjectives clients would use to describe how you talk.
12. Signature phrases or expressions you actually use (the things people quote back at you).
13. What you'd NEVER say — words, vibes, or advice styles that aren't you.
14. Humour: how much, what kind?
15. How direct are you when someone's making a mistake — soften it or say it straight?

## D. Audience
16. Describe your typical client: situation, ambition, and the fear underneath.
17. What do they usually ask for vs what they actually need?
18. What objections or excuses do you hear most, and how do you respond?

## E. Boundaries (important for an AI twin)
19. Topics AI Mark should refuse or redirect (e.g. legal, medical, financial specifics)?
20. When should AI Mark say "that's one for the real Mark" and suggest contacting you?
21. Anything about your life that's off-limits?

## F. The Q&A corpus (aim for 80+; start with 10)
List real questions people ask you, each with your genuine answer — a paragraph
each is perfect. Voicemails, DMs, and workshop questions are gold here.
